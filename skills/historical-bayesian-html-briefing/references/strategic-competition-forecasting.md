# Strategic Competition Forecasting

Use this reference when the requester asks for odds on competing with a frontier lab, startup, product category, or well-funded incumbent.

## Frame the question diagonally

Do not forecast only the dramatic head-to-head outcome. Split the problem into distinct, resolvable or semi-resolvable outcomes:

- Direct head-to-head win on the incumbent's core axis.
- Defensible niche product / wedge on a different axis.
- OSS/reputation/proof-of-work upside.
- Serious startup/revenue/acquisition outcome.
- Personal-only or stalled outcome.

State clearly when the best strategy is diagonal competition rather than imitation.

## Conditional forecasts

If the requester asks "what if I achieve every goal?", treat that as a conditional forecast:

- Keep the original unconditional/base forecast separate.
- Update with likelihood ratios for reduced execution risk.
- Do not erase adoption, distribution, timing, and incumbent-response risk.
- Report which risks remain after technical execution succeeds.

Example remaining risks after technical success:

- users understand the product category;
- install/setup is reliable;
- demos communicate the wedge in under a minute;
- the project recruits a small power-user loop;
- incumbent products do not absorb the differentiator.

## Scenario distribution pattern

Use a mutually exclusive scenario distribution alongside overlapping binary forecasts.

Example labels:

- strong personal-only system;
- OSS/reputation wedge;
- niche product;
- strong career outcome;
- startup or acquisition;
- direct incumbent competitor.

Keep these separate from overlapping event probabilities such as "career upside" or "fundable startup".

## Tone

Prefer blunt odds over reassurance. Name the bleak path and the live path. Avoid generic encouragement. If the direct fight is a bad bet, say so, then identify the sharper wedge.

## HTML artifact notes

For strategic competition pages, the briefing should include:

- headline odds for direct competition, niche product, career upside, startup outcome;
- mutually exclusive scenario cards;
- Bayesian calculation trail with priors and likelihood ratios;
- causal flow showing why the diagonal wedge differs from head-to-head competition;
- triggers that move the forecast up/down;
- embedded machine-readable JSON ledger.
